# OC Cosign

**Get behind a builder — join as a co-founder, or back them with sats.**
[cosign.ochk.io](https://cosign.ochk.io) is a commercial sub-product of
OrangeCheck, parallel to [me.ochk.io](/me/), [vault.ochk.io](/vault/), and
[chat.ochk.io](/chat/). It is a warm marketplace where Bitcoiners post what
they’re building and what they need — and everyone else can _cosign_ it.

A cosign is a public act of support that actually counts. You can:

- **join** as a co-founder,
- **back** a project as a patron (sats settle peer-to-peer), or
- **zap** a little conviction.

The pitch is one sentence: **back the builders you believe in — with your name,
your skills, or your sats — and let real conviction be the signal.**

## What OC Cosign is — and is not

|                               |                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Not a securities platform** | Backing a project is an endorsement and a patron relationship, never the purchase of a regulated security. Founders set their own terms in plain English; OC takes no equity and brokers nothing. |
| **No custody**                | Sats settle **peer-to-peer over Lightning, straight to the founder’s own Lightning address.** OC is never in the money flow and holds nothing. See [Trust & safety](/cosign/trust).               |
| **No KYC, no account**        | Your OrangeCheck identity is the account. Start in seconds with Google, GitHub, or an email one-time code — or sign in with a Bitcoin wallet for full self-custody. One identity either way.      |
| **Built for everyone**        | Founders don’t need to be engineers, and backers don’t need to run a node. The Bitcoin identity is the quiet substrate, not an obstacle course.                                                   |

## The loop

1. **Post a project** — what you’re building, and what you need (a co-founder,
   capital, or both).
2. **People cosign it** — join, back, or zap.
3. **You match** — a join or a back is a request; when you accept, a private
   thread opens (double opt-in, both sides in).
4. **You talk, then build** — message in the thread, post updates, and everyone
   behind the project gets notified.

Read [How it works](/cosign/how-it-works) for the full walkthrough, or jump to
the [Quickstart](/cosign/quickstart).

## Where it fits in the family

OC Cosign is a **product**, not a seventh verb. It stands on the same
OrangeCheck identity layer every sibling shares — the cross-subdomain auth host
at [ochk.io](https://ochk.io), one `did:oc` per person, the same sign-in
ceremony. Proving control of a Bitcoin address (the verified-Bitcoiner upgrade)
is optional and additive — never required to get in.
